about me

Physics Meets
Artificial Intelligence

I'm a Theoretical and Experimental Physicist based in Padua, Italy, affiliated with the University of Padova (UNIPD). My research lives at the intersection of quantum mechanics, computational physics, and cutting-edge machine learning.

My core focus is Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) — embedding conservation laws, physical constraints, and domain knowledge directly into neural architectures. This makes models not just accurate, but physically consistent and interpretable.

Current projects span Li-ion battery modeling, perovskite solar cell optimization, SEM image classification with Spiking Neural Networks, and DFT-based materials property prediction.

Bayesian PINN — Li-ion Battery SPM

Bayesian Physics-Informed Neural Network for the Single Particle Model of Li-ion batteries. Fuses uncertainty quantification with physics-constrained learning for robust battery state estimation.

PINNs Bayesian DL Python Battery

PIML — InSnCl3 Perovskite Solar Cells

Physics-informed ML workflow for SCAPS-1D modeling of InSnCl3 perovskite solar cells. Uses ML surrogates to accelerate device simulation and parameter optimization.

PIML Perovskites Jupyter Solar Energy

FractoSpike-SEM Image Classification

Leakage-safe SEM image classification toolkit combining CNNs, Spiking Neural Networks, and self-supervised learning. Addresses privacy-preserving ML in materials characterization workflows.

CNNs SNN Self-Supervised Python

Effective Mass Calculator

Calculates electron & hole effective masses from VASP/VASPKIT BAND.dat files using parabolic band-edge fitting. An essential tool for semiconductor and materials property analysis.

DFT VASP Jupyter Materials
